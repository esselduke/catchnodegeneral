5 workflows every business should automate in 2025

Sarah Chen
CTO & Co-Founder

After implementing automation for over 200 businesses, we've seen the same patterns emerge: certain workflows consistently deliver massive ROI, regardless of industry. Here are the five automations that save teams the most time and why they're surprisingly easy to implement.

1. Invoice Processing & Data Entry

If your team is still manually entering data from invoices into your accounting system, you're burning 10-15 hours per week on a task that can be 95% automated.

What gets automated:

  • Email monitoring for incoming invoices
  • PDF data extraction (vendor, amount, date, line items)
  • Validation against purchase orders
  • Entry into QuickBooks, Xero, or NetSuite
  • Approval routing to managers
  • Payment scheduling

Typical savings: 12 hours per week for a company processing 50+ invoices monthly

Tools needed: Power Automate or Zapier + your accounting software + email

Pro Tip

Start with a single vendor's invoices. Perfect the automation for one data source before expanding to all vendors. This approach reduces errors and builds confidence.

2. Customer Onboarding Workflows

Whether you're onboarding new clients, employees, or vendors, manual onboarding creates bottlenecks, inconsistencies, and a poor first impression. Automated onboarding ensures nothing falls through the cracks.

What gets automated:

  • Welcome email sequences
  • Document collection (W-9s, contracts, etc.)
  • Account setup in your systems
  • Training resource delivery
  • Task assignment to team members
  • Progress tracking and follow-ups

Typical savings: 3-5 hours per new customer/employee

Tools needed: CRM or HRIS + email automation + document management

3. Report Generation & Distribution

We've encountered countless businesses where someone spends every Monday morning (or Friday afternoon) pulling data from multiple systems, creating charts in Excel, and emailing reports to stakeholders. This is peak automation territory.

What gets automated:

  • Data extraction from multiple sources
  • Calculations and data transformation
  • Chart and visualization generation
  • PDF creation with branded templates
  • Scheduled email distribution
  • Archiving to SharePoint or Google Drive

Typical savings: 4-8 hours per week

Tools needed: Power BI or Tableau + Power Automate + email

4. Lead Follow-Up & Qualification

Speed matters in sales. Leads that receive a response within 5 minutes are 21x more likely to convert than those contacted after 30 minutes. Yet most businesses take hours or days to follow up. Automation fixes this.

What gets automated:

  • Instant acknowledgment emails
  • Lead scoring based on form responses
  • CRM entry and enrichment
  • Task creation for sales team
  • Nurture sequences for cold leads
  • Meeting scheduling

Typical savings: 15-20 hours per week + increased conversion rates

Tools needed: CRM + email automation + scheduling software

5. IT Support Ticket Routing

Most IT teams still manually read every support email and decide where to route it. Smart routing automation can handle 70% of this work instantly.

What gets automated:

  • Ticket creation from emails/forms
  • Categorization based on keywords
  • Priority assignment
  • Routing to specialized team members
  • Auto-responses to common questions
  • SLA tracking and escalation

Typical savings: 10-15 hours per week for IT teams

Tools needed: Ticketing system (Zendesk, Freshdesk) + automation rules

Common Pitfall

Don't try to automate broken processes. If your current workflow is inefficient, automation will just make you inefficiently faster. Fix the process first, then automate it.

Getting Started: Pick One and Implement

The biggest mistake businesses make is trying to automate everything at once. Instead:

  1. Choose the workflow causing the most pain. Look for tasks that are repetitive, time-consuming, and error-prone.
  2. Map the current process. Document every step before automating. You'll discover inefficiencies you didn't know existed.
  3. Start with a pilot. Automate one subset of the workflow (e.g., one vendor's invoices) before expanding.
  4. Measure the results. Track time saved, error reduction, and user satisfaction.
  5. Iterate and expand. Refine the automation based on feedback, then apply the same approach to other workflows.

Most businesses see ROI within 3 months on their first automation project. The time savings compound quickly as you tackle additional workflows.
